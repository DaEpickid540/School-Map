Before You Start

You'll need Safari on your iPhone — this only works through Safari, not Chrome or other browsers. Make sure you're connected to Wi-Fi when you first install it.

Step-by-step instructions
1
Open Safari & navigate to the kiosk
Open Safari on your iPhone and go to the Mason Navigator URL. You can get this by scanning the QR code shown on the kiosk display at school, or by typing the address directly.

2
Tap the Share button
At the bottom of Safari, tap the Share button — it looks like a box with an arrow pointing up (↑). If you don't see the toolbar, scroll up slightly on the page to reveal it.

💡

The Share button is the middle icon in the Safari bottom bar — a square with an upward arrow. It's not the same as the bookmark icon.

3
Hit the 3 dots "More" option then hit "Add to Home Screen"
A menu will slide up from the bottom. Scroll down through the options until you see "Add to Home Screen" with a plus icon. Tap it.
📷

"Add to Home Screen" option visible

💡

This option is below the row of app icons in the share sheet. You may need to scroll down a bit — it's usually about halfway down the list.

4
Name it and tap "Add"
A screen will appear letting you name the app. It should already say "Mason Navigator" — leave it as-is or shorten it if you prefer. Then tap Add in the top-right corner.
📷

5
Find the icon on your Home Screen
Mason Navigator will appear as an icon on your iPhone Home Screen — just like a regular app. It'll have the school icon and the name you chose. Press the Home button (or swipe up) to go back and find it.
📷

6
Open it — it works like a real app!
Tap the icon to launch Mason Navigator. It opens full screen, with no Safari address bar or tabs — just the app. Once loaded, it works even without a network connection.
📷

⚡

Route links from the kiosk's QR code expire after 30 minutes — so scan and open them right away. The installed app itself never expires.

🎉

You're all set!

Mason Navigator is now on your Home Screen. Scan any QR code from a kiosk to instantly load your route.
